Portfolio 5 - Openness and the Big Five in Emerging Adulthood

Goal

Expand on my previous portfolio piece looking at a sample from mTurk to observe patterns to further my future work on openness to experience. This portfolio piece will focus to test how well other Big Five traits (extraversion, agreeableness, conscientiousness, and emotional stability) predict openness to experience, and examine whether mean levels and predictors of openness are similar across emerging‑adult age groups (18–23, 23–25, 25–30) that parallel the target ages in my planned chatbot study.

Products

This project will produce a cleaned Big Five dataset with TIPI‑based composite scores for extraversion, agreeableness, conscientiousness, emotional stability, and openness. I will create a correlation table showing how openness relates to the other four traits in the full MTurk sample, followed by a multiple regression model predicting openness from extraversion, agreeableness, conscientiousness, and emotional stability with standardized coefficients and overall model fit. I will derive age variables that capture the emerging‑adult bands 18–23, 23–25, and 25–30, and use these to compute and visualize mean openness and confidence intervals across age bands. Finally, I will run parallel regressions predicting openness within each age band and summarize them in a compact table, allowing me to compare whether the pattern of trait predictors is similar or different across emerging‑adult ages that map onto the target range for my planned chatbot study.

library(dplyr)

big5_summ <- big5 %>%
  group_by(age_18_23) %>%
  summarise(
    n   = n(),
    mean_open = mean(openness, na.rm = TRUE),
    sd_open   = sd(openness, na.rm = TRUE),
    se_open   = sd_open / sqrt(n),
    ci_low    = mean_open - 1.96 * se_open,
    ci_high   = mean_open + 1.96 * se_open
  )

ggplot(big5_summ,
       aes(x = age_18_23, y = mean_open)) +
  geom_point(size = 3) +
  geom_errorbar(aes(ymin = ci_low, ymax = ci_high),
                width = 0.1, size = 0.9) +
  labs(x = "Age group",
       y = "Mean openness",
       title = "Openness levels in 18–23 vs 24+ adults") +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 12)

Interpretation: Mean openness scores were similar for emerging adults (18–23; wide confidence interval) and adults aged 24 and older, suggesting that openness levels in my planned college‑aged sample are broadly comparable to those observed in older adults in this MTurk dataset.
